Nick Pranske Final Project

Welcome to my creative component of my final project! The rules of the game are simple: drag the word to the language you think it was borrowed from. You see, Hawaiian Pidgin is a mix of English, Cantonese, Hawaiian, and Portuguse. It also has some Japanese influence. So, let's see how good you are at identifying the root language of these words and phrases!

  1. "Da stew stei nice." means "The stew is good." and is Portuguese

  2. "Hiz haus ste da mauka said." means "His house is on the mountainside" and comes from Hawaiian

  3. "Buckaloose" means "Go crazy" and is English

  4. "Nomo kaukau in da haus" means "No more food in the house", which comes from "chow chow" originating in Cantonese

  5. "Bachi" is a term for "Punishment/Retaliation" in Japanese

  6. "Ono da malasadaz." - malasadaz are Portuguese fried dough balls

  7. "Ste chraing fo ak lokol." means "He's trying to act local" in English

  8. "Char siu" means "Barbequed pork" in Cantonese
